What it is

A working telecom BSS assembled from small, standards-conformant services — each exposing a TM Forum Open API, each owning its own database, all behind one gateway. Nothing is operator-specific: any OIDC identity provider, any PostgreSQL, any Kafka-protocol broker. Two demo operators (one English/EUR, one Norwegian/NOK) run side by side on a single deployment to prove the multi-tenancy.

  • Commerce: TMF620 catalog, ordering, inventory, cart, qualification, appointments, stock, agreements, promotions — soft bundles, configured pricing, no SKU explosion.
  • Money: a crash-resumable billing run, seven e-invoice formats as config, remittance by camt.054 / OCR giro / BAI2, a tokenized payment vault.
  • Marketing & growth (built in, not bolted on): a full martech stack inside the BSS — event-triggered campaign journeys, A/B arms with an honest statistical verdict, and holdout-measured lift so you prove a campaign worked instead of claiming it; a per-tenant email seam with delivery receipts and bounce suppression, frequency caps and quiet hours, GA4 audience import and Meta-shaped Custom Audience / Lead Ads sync, and a TMF699 lead-to-opportunity sales funnel — no separate marketing cloud required.
  • Personalization, consent-first: a "For you" rail ranked from the customer's own data, next-hit session reaction (the last thing you looked at shapes the next page), and "customers who bought this also bought" market-basket affinity — none of it collected or shown without consent.
  • AI, governed: an AI control plane — every LLM turn metered, budgeted, kill-switchable and audited — under product/CSR copilots, a churn-prediction engine, and AI-authored personalization rules an operator can chat into existence.
  • Care & production: trouble tickets, an omnichannel interaction record, thin service orchestration and assurance, number porting.

Proven, not promised

Sixty-three Playwright suites drive real browsers across every surface; eleven official TM Forum Conformance Test Kits pass with zero failures; three production hardening waves are backed by live proofs (tick locks under two replicas, a backup that provably restores, GDPR rights as working endpoints); and the same Helm chart has run on local Kubernetes, AWS EKS and Azure AKS — the same application, every cloud difference absorbed by a seam. The honest gaps are written down, not hidden.

Post-quantum ready (not "quantum-proof")

The BSS is crypto-agile by construction — identity, transport and payment crypto all live behind seams, so migrating to post-quantum is swapping what a seam points at, not a rewrite. Today: SIM secrets are AES-256-GCM at rest, PKCE is SHA-256, and a hybrid X25519MLKEM768 TLS key exchange (ML-KEM-768, FIPS 203) guards recorded traffic against harvest-now-decrypt-later. The one classically vulnerable primitive — RSA token signatures at the identity provider — is named honestly and swaps to ML-DSA at the identity seam when the OIDC ecosystem lands it.
