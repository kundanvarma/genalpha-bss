The mobile app

Design proposal v2 — a MyJio-class modular app: “the app assembles itself from your BSS” · React Native (Expo)

The core idea: LOB modules, not fixed screens

Inspired by MyJio's super-app model (mobile, fiber, TV, engagement in one app), the app is a composition of line-of-business modules — mobile, broadband, TV, and future verticals like energy — assembled at runtime from three levels of configuration:

LevelDecidesDriven by
OperatorWhich LOB modules exist at allThe ODA composer: deployed components + catalog categories, served in the gateway's tenant manifest with branding tokens
CustomerWhich modules show, in what orderTMF637 inventory: what they own becomes a live card; what they lack becomes a TMF680 cross-sell slot
CatalogNew verticals without an app releaseTMF620 categories + TMF635 usage types — kWh rates exactly like GB; energy is a catalog entry plus a module, not a new system

Design principles

  • Care first, shop second. Usage, bill and help are one tap from launch; every LOB card carries its own cross-sell slot instead of a separate store push.
  • One binary, every operator, every household shape. A mobile-only customer, a triple-play family and (later) an energy customer open visibly different apps — same code, different BSS data.
  • Nothing new on the backend. Every module below is powered by APIs that exist and are E2E-tested today.
  • Engagement-ready. Campaigns and gamification (missions, streaks, rewards) are the planned martech engine reading the tenant-tagged event stream and paying out through TMF671 promotions — the app reserves the surface for it from day one.
  • Same auth model: OIDC code + PKCE in the system browser, against the tenant's issuer. Push notifications and biometric unlock are native-phase additions.

Engagement layer (campaigns & gamification) — reserved, phased

MyJio's stickiness comes from engagement, and the substrate is already live here: every business moment is a tenant-tagged event, promotions are the reward vehicle, TMF681 the delivery channel. The planned martech engine turns those into campaigns (“abandoned cart + 7 days → push WELCOME10”) and missions (“enable autopay → 10% off next bill”, usage streaks, badges). The app reserves a Home slot and a Rewards surface for it from day one, so the engine lands as a module — not a redesign.

Build approach

Expo (React Native). One codebase targeting iOS, Android and web. The web target is the autonomous-verification trick: the whole app gets a Playwright E2E suite exactly like the five web-channel suites, runnable tonight without Xcode or Android SDKs. When native tooling is available on the Mac, the identical code runs on simulators/devices — nothing is rewritten. Phase 2 (native-only): push notifications from the TMF681 stream, biometric unlock.
