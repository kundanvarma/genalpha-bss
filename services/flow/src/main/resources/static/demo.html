The guided demo — genalpha-bss tells its own story

every step below is REAL: live API calls against the running BSS · watch the processes light up on the right · process view →
Five acts, one running system. Each act narrates what a telecom does, then does it for real — orders, rules, prices, numbers — while Live Flow (right) shows the components reacting. Nothing is mocked on this page: if the stack were down, every act would fail.

Act 1 A customer orders — and the machine wakes up

A brand-new customer buys a mobile plan. The order decomposes into network work, a phone number is drawn from the operator's pool, the service activates, and the customer is notified — no human touches anything.

⬤ watch “Order → Activate” light up on the right

Act 2 A business rule, born without a deployment

The operator decides: max 2 of any item per order. We author that rule as data — a row, not code — and the very next order obeys it. Then we switch it off and the same order sails through. The deployment never moved.

Act 3 A price that reacts to who you are

Marketing wants a 15% launch discount. Same engine, second trick: the rule adjusts prices instead of blocking orders. The storefront cart previews it, the bill applies it — we’ll ask the pricing engine directly and watch €100 become €85.

Act 4 Keep your number

A customer joins from a rival and wants to keep their number. The port-in goes through the national clearinghouse (NRDB in Norway), the cutover completes, and when their plan activates it carries their own number — no pool draw.

⬤ watch “Port-in → Keep your number” light up on the right

Act 5 Leave with it — and teach the machine

The truest test of an open platform: the same customer leaves. The port-out ceases the service, releases the number — and the departure becomes a labelled churn outcome the AI learns from. Losing a customer teaches the system to keep the next one.

⬤ watch “Port-out → Leave” light up on the right
That’s the story. One deployment, thirty-one components, every step you just saw verified by the same APIs the channels use.
